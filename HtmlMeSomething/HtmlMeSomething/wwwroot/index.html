Oh my dog! The best practices for dog owners

By Sarah Park

Welcome to my dog blog! Below are some helpful ways to help keep your pup safe and happy.



Harmful Toxins

Most dog owners know that chocolate can be deadly to dogs. But did you know how many others foods there are that dogs should never eat? Even more dangerous than chocolate are onions, garlic, avacado, grapes, and raisins.

Will you have most likely heard of giving a dog a bone, you should never actually do this. Animal bones can splinter and either block or cut your dog's digestive system.

The most deadly of all? Xylitol. This is often found in gum, candy, diet sweetners, and toothpaste. Make sure to keep these common products, along with the foods listed above, out of reach of your dog at all times.


Weather Hazards

Hot weather doesn't just affect you. Some summertime hazards you should be careful of are heat stroke and sunburn. If the concrete is too hot for your bare feet, it is too hot for your dogs paws!

Cold weather can be just as dangerous. Be careful to keep your dogs paws safe from the ice and snow which can cause frostbite. Most sidewalk salt is also harmful to dogs paws which can absorb the harmful toxins. Most stores sell dog safe sidewalk salt, but beware that most homes and stores will not when you are taking your dog on walks.


Safe Driving

Car rides are necessary for all pet owners, whether going to the vet or around town, so making sure our dogs are safe in the car is important. If we are expected to wear seat belts, why wouldn't our dogs? There are very inexpensive harnesses for dogs that hook into the seat belt hook in cars.

Just like you should never leave a child in a car alone, you should also never leave your dog. Even on a 78℉ day, the temperature in a parked car can get up to 100℉ to 120℉ in minutes. Imagine how hot it would get if it were a 90℉ day!

Be advised: this article is based on general knowledge and opinion, not facts.
